   - Determine whether the patient should be treated as a cardiac patient or a general population patient based on clinical history/context.
   - Cardiac patient examples: CAD, HF, post-MI, cardiac rehabilitation, chronic cardiovascular disease.

   - Relationship: In cardiac patients, an increasing RHR trend may indicate cardiovascular deconditioning, worsening heart failure, poor rate control, or emerging acute illness.
   - Scope: Apply to adults only; use cardiac-specific thresholds for CAD / HF / post-MI patients and general thresholds for the general population.
   - Cardiac patients:

     - AMBER: RHR 40‚Äď60 bpm or 70‚Äď100 bpm if clinically explained, or mild upward trend over time.
     - HIGH RISK: RHR ‚Č• 70 bpm in cardiac patients with persistent elevation, RHR increase ‚Č• 10 bpm over 7 days, or RHR ‚Č§ 55 bpm suggesting possible overtreatment.

   - General population:
     - GREEN: AHR 60‚Äď100 bpm, RHR 40‚Äď100 bpm, and stable or decreasing RHR trend.
     - AMBER: Mildly abnormal RHR that may be explained by activity, stress, or illness.
     - HIGH RISK: RHR increase ‚Č• 5 bpm over 5 years, or persistent resting tachycardia that is clinically concerning.
     - RED: HR < 40 bpm or > 160 bpm, or sudden change ‚Č• 30 bpm within minutes.
   - Interpretation:

   - Clinical action:

‚Äá2.‚Ā† ‚Ā†Parameter: Step Count

   - Scope: Apply to adults only; use cardiac-specific thresholds for CAD / HF / post-MI patients and general thresholds for the general population.
   - Cardiac patients:
     - GREEN: ‚Č• 6,500 steps/day.
     - AMBER: 3,000‚Äď6,500 steps/day.
     - HIGH RISK: < 3,000 steps/day.

   - General population:
     - GREEN: ‚Č• 10,000 steps/day.
     - AMBER: 5,000‚Äď7,000 steps/day.

   - Clinical action:
     - If sudden step-count drop > 50%, assess for fatigue, intercurrent illness, decompensation, or device/data issues.
     - If step count remains persistently low, recommend physiotherapist or rehabilitation intervention where appropriate.

‚Äá6.‚Ā† ‚Ā†Combined Risk Patterns

   - Arrhythmia suspicion pattern:

   - If ‚Č• 2 HIGH RISK findings or any RED finding are present, classify overall risk as RED.

‚Äá7.‚Ā† ‚Ā†Longitudinal Behaviour Pattern
   - If there is no improvement in steps over repeated monitoring and activity remains low, record an escalating risk category.
   - If two parameters improve over time, record meaningful positive change.
   - Use trend information to support interpretation, not just single-point thresholds.

‚Äá8.‚Ā† ‚Ā†Overall Risk Logic

     - Step count
     - Sedentary time
     - Physical activity
   - Then evaluate combined patterns.
   - Rules:

   - Cardiac patients:
